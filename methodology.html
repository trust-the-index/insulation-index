Methodology

What is the EE Index?
The Entitlement & Excess (EE) Index visualizes neighborhood characteristics across the United States using publicly available data. Unlike other platforms that stigmatize resource-deprived communities, the EE Index highlights areas of extreme resource hoarding and social insulation.

The EE Index Score

The EE Index is calculated by aggregating "Points of Exclusion" into H3 hexagonal grid cells. A higher score (80-100) indicates an area with a high concentration of exclusionary indicators, commonly referred to as a "Manager Zone."

0 (COMMUNITY DRIVEN) 100 (HIGH ENTITLEMENT)

Geographic Grid System

To ensure statistical accuracy, we utilize Uber’s H3 Hexagonal Grid system. This provides consistent cell sizes across the map, ensuring that "Entitlement Clusters" are accurately contained without edge effects from square grids.
